## Team Structure Overview

OptiBio's Phase 1 team is designed for maximum efficiency with minimal overhead. Each role has clear ownership, but success requires tight coordination across functions.

**The 5-Person Team:**

1. **Performance Marketing Specialist** - Drives customer acquisition
2. **Conversion-Focused Designer** - Optimizes every pixel for conversion
3. **Customer Success / Operations** - Delights customers and manages logistics
4. **Content Strategist / Copywriter** - Crafts persuasive, science-backed messaging
5. **Fractional CFO / Finance Advisor** - Ensures healthy unit economics and cash flow

**Reporting Structure:**
- All 5 roles report directly to Founder
- No middle management (flat structure)
- Autonomous execution within defined authority
- Weekly sync to align priorities

## Core Workflows

### Workflow 1: New Ad Campaign Launch

**Trigger:** Performance Marketing identifies opportunity for new campaign

**Process:**

**Day 1 (Monday):** Performance Marketing posts brief
- Campaign objective (acquire 100 new customers at <$30 CAC)
- Target audience (women 25-35, health-conscious, high stress)
- Budget allocation ($2,000 over 2 weeks)
- Creative needs (10 ad variations, 3 angles)

**Day 2 (Tuesday):** Content Strategist writes copy
- 10 ad copy variations (scarcity, social proof, benefits angles)
- Headlines, body copy, CTAs for each variation
- Posts in shared doc for review

**Day 3 (Wednesday):** Designer creates ad creative
- Design 10 ad variations matching copy
- Mobile-first layouts (60% of traffic)
- Brand-consistent visuals (navy, ivory, gold)
- Posts in shared folder for review

**Day 4 (Thursday):** Performance Marketing launches
- Upload ads to Meta Ads Manager
- Set targeting, budget, schedule
- Configure conversion tracking
- Launch campaign by EOD

**Day 5-11 (Fri-Thu):** Performance Marketing optimizes
- Monitor daily performance
- Pause losers (CPA >$50)
- Scale winners (increase budget 20%)
- Report daily metrics in standup

**Day 12 (Friday):** Team reviews results
- Performance Marketing shares results (spend, CAC, ROAS)
- Content Strategist analyzes copy performance (which angles won?)
- Designer analyzes creative performance (which visuals won?)
- Fractional CFO validates unit economics (is CAC profitable?)
- Team decides: Scale? Pivot? Kill?

**Outcome:** Data-driven decision on campaign future

---

### Workflow 2: Conversion Rate Optimization

**Trigger:** Conversion rate plateaus or drops

**Process:**

**Day 1 (Monday):** Designer analyzes funnel
- Review analytics (where are users dropping off?)
- Check heatmaps (where are users clicking? where are they stuck?)
- Watch session recordings (what's confusing?)
- Identify bottleneck (product page? cart? checkout?)

**Day 2 (Tuesday):** Designer + Content Strategist brainstorm
- Hypothesize reasons for drop-off (not compelling? too expensive? confusing?)
- Design 3-5 A/B test ideas (new headline? urgency indicator? trust badges?)
- Prioritize based on impact potential and effort

**Day 3 (Wednesday):** Content Strategist writes new copy
- Write new headlines, bullet points, CTAs
- Test different angles (benefits vs. features, urgency vs. social proof)
- Posts in shared doc for review

**Day 4 (Thursday):** Designer implements A/B test
- Create new page variation in Shopify
- Set up A/B test (50/50 traffic split)
- Configure conversion tracking
- Launch test by EOD

**Day 5-18 (2 weeks):** Monitor test performance
- Designer checks daily (is test running correctly?)
- Performance Marketing monitors traffic (enough visitors for significance?)
- Fractional CFO validates impact (is lift worth the effort?)

**Day 19 (Friday):** Team reviews results
- Designer shares results (conversion rate lift, statistical significance)
- If winner: Implement permanently
- If loser: Document learnings, design next test
- Fractional CFO updates financial model (new conversion rate assumption)

**Outcome:** Continuous conversion rate improvement

### Workflow 3: Customer Issue Escalation

**Trigger:** Customer Success encounters issue requiring team input

**Process:**

**Immediate (within 1 hour):** Customer Success triages
- Assess severity (minor inconvenience? safety concern? legal threat?)
- Determine if within authority (can I solve this myself?)
- If yes: Solve immediately, document in weekly report
- If no: Escalate to appropriate team member or founder

**Escalation Paths:**

**Product quality issue** ‚Üí Escalate to Founder
- Example: Customer reports adverse reaction, contamination, or safety concern
- Action: Founder coordinates with manufacturer, decides on recall/refund

**Shipping/fulfillment issue** ‚Üí Customer Success resolves
- Example: Lost package, wrong address, damaged in transit
- Action: Send replacement immediately, file claim with 3PL

**Website/technical issue** ‚Üí Escalate to Designer or Performance Marketing
- Example: Checkout broken, page not loading, mobile issue
- Action: Designer fixes immediately, tests across devices

**Pricing/refund issue** ‚Üí Escalate to Fractional CFO
- Example: Customer disputes charge, requests bulk discount
- Action: CFO advises on pricing policy, approves exceptions

**Marketing/messaging issue** ‚Üí Escalate to Content Strategist
- Example: Customer confused by ad claim, wants scientific proof
- Action: Content Strategist provides clinical studies, updates copy if needed

**Follow-up (within 24 hours):** Customer Success closes loop
- Update customer with resolution
- Document issue in weekly report
- Share learnings with team (how do we prevent this?)

**Outcome:** Fast resolution, continuous improvement

**When:** Every Monday, 10am, 30 minutes

- Customer Success: "Customers confused about dosage, need clearer instructions"
- Content Strategist: "Science angle underperforms vs. emotional benefits"
- Fractional CFO: "Inventory turns are slow, need to forecast better"

**4. This Week's Priorities (10 minutes)** - Each person shares top 3 priorities
- Performance Marketing: 
  1. Scale winning Facebook ads to $1K/day
  2. Launch Google Search campaign
  3. Test 3 new creative angles
- Designer:
  1. Fix mobile checkout friction
  2. Design 10 new ad variations
  3. Run product page urgency test
- Customer Success:
  1. Update dosage instructions on product page
  2. Collect 10 new testimonials
  3. Coordinate with 3PL on inventory restock
- Content Strategist:
  1. Write 15 new ad copy variations (emotional angle)

  1. Update financial model with new conversion rate

### Workflow 5: Monthly Performance Review

**When:** First Monday of every month, 60 minutes

**Attendees:** All 5 team members + Founder

**Agenda:**

**1. Founder Vision Update (10 minutes)**
- Strategic direction (are we pivoting? doubling down?)
- Big decisions made last month
- What's coming next month

**2. Performance Deep Dive (20 minutes)** - Fractional CFO presents
- Full financial review (revenue, expenses, profit/loss)
- Unit economics (CAC, LTV, contribution margin, payback period)
- Cohort analysis (which channels have best LTV?)
- Cash flow forecast (runway, burn rate, fundraising needs)
- Are we on track for quarterly goals?

**3. Team Showcase (20 minutes)** - Each person presents best work
- Performance Marketing: Campaign that drove most revenue
- Designer: A/B test with biggest conversion lift
- Customer Success: Customer story that made us proud
- Content Strategist: Copy that performed best
- Fractional CFO: Financial insight that changed strategy

**4. Strategic Planning (5 minutes)** - Founder leads
- What are next month's priorities?
- Any big bets or experiments?
- Resource allocation (budget, time, focus)

**5. Team Feedback (5 minutes)** - Open discussion
- What's working well?
- What should we change?
- Any process improvements?

**Outcome:** Alignment on strategy, celebration of wins, continuous improvement

## Decision-Making Framework

### Level 1: Individual Authority (No Approval Needed)

**Performance Marketing:**
- Daily budget shifts <$200
- Pause/launch ads based on performance
- Creative testing (new angles, copy, images)
- Audience targeting adjustments

**Designer:**
- Ad creative variations
- Landing page A/B tests (within brand guidelines)
- Email template designs
- Product page optimizations

**Customer Success:**
- Refunds <$100
- Replacement orders (damaged/lost)
- Shipping upgrades
- Small customer gestures (bonus gift, handwritten note)

**Content Strategist:**
- Ad copy variations
- Email subject lines and body copy
- Product page copy tweaks
- Blog post topics and content

**Fractional CFO:**
- Bookkeeping and accounting processes
- Financial reporting formats
- Metrics definitions
- Scenario modeling assumptions

---

### Level 2: Team Consensus (Discuss Before Deciding)

**Examples:**
- Major conversion rate optimization (homepage redesign)
- New marketing channel launch (TikTok, Pinterest, Reddit)
- Pricing experiments (test $59.99 vs. $49.99)
- Email sequence changes (welcome series, abandoned cart)
- Product page messaging pivot (science vs. emotional benefits)

**Process:**
1. Person proposing posts in Slack with context and recommendation
2. Team discusses (pros, cons, risks, alternatives)
3. Team votes or reaches consensus
4. Person proposing implements decision
5. Team reviews results and iterates

---

### Level 3: Founder Approval (Escalate Before Deciding)

**Examples:**
- Budget increases (>$10K total spend)
- Major strategy pivots (change positioning, target audience)
- Product changes (new SKUs, pricing changes)
- Partnership decisions (influencer deals >$1K)
- Legal/compliance issues (refund policy, terms of service)
- Hiring decisions (new team members)
- Fundraising decisions (raise capital? how much? from whom?)

**Process:**
1. Person proposing writes brief (1-page summary)
2. Include: Context, recommendation, alternatives, risks
3. Send to founder for review
4. Founder schedules call if needed
5. Founder approves, rejects, or requests more info
6. Person implements decision

## Conflict Resolution

### Step 1: Direct Communication

If two team members disagree, they should talk directly first (Slack DM or call).

**Example:**
- Performance Marketing wants to spend $5K on TikTok
- Fractional CFO thinks it's too risky (unproven channel)
- They discuss: What's the hypothesis? What's the downside? What's the test plan?

---

### Step 2: Data-Driven Discussion

If direct communication doesn't resolve, bring data to the discussion.

**Example:**
- Performance Marketing shares: TikTok CPMs are 50% lower than Facebook
- Fractional CFO shares: But TikTok conversion rate is unknown, could be 2x worse
- Both agree: Test with $1K first, measure conversion rate, then decide

---

### Step 3: Founder Tie-Breaker

If data doesn't resolve, escalate to founder for final decision.

**Example:**
- Performance Marketing and Fractional CFO still disagree on TikTok
- Founder decides: Test with $1K, but only if Facebook CAC >$40 (need alternative channel)

## Team Culture & Values

### 1. Bias Toward Action

**What it means:** When in doubt, do something. Learn by doing, not by planning.

**In practice:**
- Launch imperfect ads and iterate (don't wait for perfect creative)
- Test pricing changes (don't debate for weeks)
- Ship A/B tests (don't overthink hypothesis)

**Red flag:** Analysis paralysis, endless meetings, "let's think about it"

---

### 2. Data-Driven Decisions

**What it means:** Use metrics to guide decisions, not gut feelings or opinions.

**In practice:**
- Review A/B test results before declaring winner
- Track CAC, LTV, conversion rate daily
- Use cohort analysis to evaluate channel performance

**Red flag:** "I think this will work" without data, ignoring test results

---

### 3. Customer Obsession

**What it means:** Every decision should improve customer experience.

**In practice:**
- Respond to support tickets within 2 hours
- Collect testimonials and reviews regularly
- Use customer language in marketing copy

**Red flag:** Ignoring customer feedback, slow response times, defensive about complaints

---

### 4. Radical Transparency

**What it means:** Share information openly, admit mistakes, ask for help.

**In practice:**
- Post daily standups (even if you had a bad day)
- Share failures in weekly reports (what didn't work)
- Ask for help when stuck (don't suffer in silence)

**Red flag:** Hiding problems, blaming others, pretending everything is fine

---

### 5. Continuous Improvement

**What it means:** Always be learning, testing, and optimizing.

**In practice:**
- Run 2-3 A/B tests at all times
- Document learnings (what did we learn this week?)
- Share knowledge with team (teach each other)

**Red flag:** "This is how we've always done it", no testing, no learning

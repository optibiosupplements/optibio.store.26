### UX Laws Successfully Applied:

1. **Fitts's Law** ✓
   - Large CTA buttons (min 44px touch targets)
   - Pre-Order button is prominent and easily clickable
   - Sticky header CTA accessible on scroll

2. **Hick's Law** ✓
   - Limited to 3 bundle choices (1, 3, 6 bottles)
   - Pre-selected "Best Value" option (3 bottles)
   - Clear visual hierarchy reduces decision time

3. **Jakob's Law** ✓
   - Familiar e-commerce patterns
   - Standard navigation placement
   - Expected checkout flow

4. **Miller's Law** ✓
   - Information chunked into 3 trust badges
   - 3 bundle options (within 7±2 rule)
   - Grouped related information

5. **Peak-End Rule** ✓
   - Golden glow effect creates memorable product presentation
   - 90-day guarantee as strong ending message
   - Social proof card as positive reinforcement

6. **Serial Position Effect** ✓
   - Trust badge at start (SCIENCE-BACKED)
   - 90-day guarantee at end
   - Key benefits in headline position

7. **Von Restorff Effect** ✓
   - "BEST VALUE" badge stands out in gold
   - PRE-ORDER PRICE badge in red
   - Selected bundle highlighted with blue border

8. **Zeigarnik Effect** ✓
   - Countdown timer creates urgency
   - Progress indication in sticky header
   - "Limited time" messaging

9. **Aesthetic-Usability Effect** ✓
   - Premium glassmorphism on sticky header
   - Soft shadows and depth
   - Golden glow effect on product
   - Consistent premium color palette

10. **Law of Proximity** ✓
    - Price and CTA grouped together
    - Trust badges grouped in row
    - Related information in cards

11. **Law of Similarity** ✓
    - Consistent button styling
    - Uniform card designs
    - Matching icon treatments

12. **Law of Common Region** ✓
    - Offer card contains all purchase info
    - Social proof in separate card
    - Trust badges in defined row
